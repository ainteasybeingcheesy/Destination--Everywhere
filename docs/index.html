Destination: Everywhere

A guilt trip.

A lesson in dogged perseverance and lockdown boredom. After the soul crushing disappointment of 2020, I have created a by no means exhaustive list of future travel plans. Will Fitz indulge my flights of fancy? Will Dave allow the time off? Only time will tell.

Jordan

Channel your inner Indiana Jones at Petra, an ancient city carved from rose-red sandstone. Camp out with the Bedouins in the Wadi Rum desert. Become at one with the water and float on the Dead Sea.

More
Philippines

With over 7000 islands to choose from, take your time to choose your favourite. And when you have found it, don’t forget to swim with the whale sharks.

More
Nepal

This small, landlocked country packs a mighty punch. Climb the highest mountains in the world all while eating a lot of Dal Bhat and drinking Everest beer.

More
Japan

Spend some time getting lost in the bustling streets of Tokyo then take the world famous bullet train to Kyoto to say hi to the monkeys who live in the bamboo forest of Arashiyama.

More
Tanzania and Zanzibar

If wildlife spotting in the Serengeti isn’t your thing, pop on over to Zanzibar where white sand beaches and crystal clear waters are waiting for you.

More
Brazil

From the Amazon, to the beaches of Rio de Janeiro. Go further north to spend carnival in Salvador, a city steeped in Afro-Brazilian heritage.

More
Russia

Spend your days visiting the Kremlin and St Basil’s Cathedral and your nights drinking Vodka. If you have the opportunity, take a trip all the way to Mongolia on the Trans-Siberian Express, also known as the Vodka train.

More
Myanmar

This small South East Asian country formerly known as Burma is famous for the ancient city of Bagan. Take a hot air balloon trip to fully experience the 2000+ Buddhist monuments that have existed for hundreds of years.

More
Turkey

More than just package holidays. Istanbul, which straddles both Europe and Asia, is peppered with beautiful mosques and rambling street markets. Head further inland to see ancient cave dwellings that are now hotels in Cappadocia.
